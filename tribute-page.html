Andrés Bonifacio y de Castro

Founder of Katipunan

His Epic Timeline

  • November 30, 1863 | Born in Tondo, Manila and the first among the six children of Catalina de Castro
  • July 7, 1892 | Officially founded the Katipunan
  • March 1893 | He met and marry his second wife Gregoria de Jesus
  • Early 1896 | They had their first son but died early because of small pox
  • August 19, 1896 | Spaniards confirmed the existence of the Katipunan
  • August 29, 1896 | He appointed generals to lead the rebel forces in Manila
  • August 30, 1896 | He personally led an attack on San Juan del Monte to block the supply on Manila
  • September to October 1896 | He supervised the establishment of Katipunan mountain and hill bases
  • November 7, 1896 | He led an assault on San Mateo, Marikina and Montalban
  • November 1896 | He commissioned Julio Nakpil to compose a national anthem - known as Marangal na Dalit ng Katagalugan
  • December 31, 1896 | He, the Magdalo and Magdiwang leaders held a meeting in Imus to determine the leadership of Cavite to end the rivalry
  • March 22, 1897 | He and the other revolutionary leaders held an important meeting to resolve the issue between the Magdalo and Madiwang forces.
  • March 23, 1897 | He and his remaining supporters gave their reasons for not accepting the result of the election
  • April 1897 | He was under arrest ordered by Aguinaldo after he received a letter alleging that he had burned down a village and church after the townspeople refused to give him provisions
  • May 10, 1897 | He and his brothers were executed despite the lack of evidence
